Who am I?

Life is full of inefficiencies, I like to fix them.

My fascination with improving the world around me began in childhood, not just questioning why things are the way they are but envisioning how they could be better. This intrinsic motivation naturally steered me toward a blend of engineering and entrepreneurship. My early ventures ranged from constructing computers and processors on breadboards to creating internet-connected home appliances, alongside starting businesses focused on homemade dog treats and innovative phone accessories (among many others that failed).

My latest endeavor stems from a familiar frustration: watching fellow developers spend countless hours crafting portfolios instead of building meaningful projects. This observation led me to join forces with two other developers to create CodeFolio, a solution that transforms how developers showcase their work. The idea emerged from the same spirit that drove my childhood tinkering - seeing an inefficiency and wondering "why can't this be simpler?" By connecting the tools developers already use, we're eliminating redundant work and letting creators focus on what they do best: creating.
